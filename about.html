Principal Investigator

Dr. Yan Kei Chiang

Lecturer · School of Engineering and Technology · UNSW Canberra

Dr. Chiang's research sits at the intersection of classical acoustics, wave physics, and engineering application. Her work asks a deceptively simple question: what can the structure of a material tell you about the signals passing through it?

Drawing on phononic crystal theory, signal processing, and machine learning, she builds both the physical structures that encode wave information and the computational pipelines that decode it. Her work combines theoretical modelling, numerical simulation, laboratory experiments, and prototype development. A central goal of her research is to translate advanced wave physics into practical technologies for sensing, diagnostics, and wave control.

The lab maintains one of the most complete acoustic measurement environments in Australia — including a full anechoic chamber, vibration laboratory, parallel waveguide system, and ultrasonic diagnostic suite — enabling research that bridges fundamental wave physics with problems in structural health monitoring, manufacturing inspection, and sensing system design.

Research Vision

Waves are more than disturbances — they are carriers of energy, information, and interaction. At WAVE Lab, we aim to understand how waves behave in complex structures and environments, and how they can be engineered for useful functions. By combining wave physics with modern sensing, computation, and experimental design, we seek to develop compact, efficient, and intelligent wave-based technologies.
